# Foodly — Product Context

**register:** brand (landing/marketing surfaces) · product (role dashboards)

## Product Purpose
Foodly is an online food-ordering platform connecting four roles: **customers** who
order, **restaurants** who cook, **riders** who deliver, and **support agents** who
help. Originally a 4-year-old PHP/AJAX project, now being revamped frontend-first
as a portfolio centerpiece. The redesign must read as intentional, crafted, and
modern — the kind of work that makes a recruiter stop scrolling.

## Users
- **Customer** — hungry, impatient, on mobile or desktop. Wants to find food and
  order in seconds. Judges trust by how the site *looks and feels*.
- **Restaurant** — manages menu, sees incoming orders. Needs clarity, not flash.
- **Rider** — picks up and delivers, checks assigned orders and streak.
- **Support agent** — handles chat with clients.

## Brand & Tone
Appetite-forward and editorial. Foodly feels like a beautifully art-directed food
magazine that happens to take orders. Confident, warm, a little indulgent. Speaks
in short, punchy, sensory phrases ("Crave. Tap. Devoured."). Never corporate,
never cutesy. The food is the hero; the UI frames it like a gallery.

## Anti-references (what Foodly must NOT look like)
- **Swiggy / Zomato / DoorDash orange-and-red** delivery-app reflex. No loud
  orange gradients, no generic deal-coupon clutter.
- **Bootstrap-default** cards, blue buttons, and flat gray everything.
- **Generic SaaS** hero-metric templates and identical icon-card grids.
- The original Foodly: inline styles, plain backgrounds, system-default type.

## Strategic Principles
1. **Photography leads.** Real food imagery is the centerpiece, framed generously.
2. **One confident accent.** Deep terracotta against a warm cream/charcoal base.
   Committed editorial palette, not a rainbow.
3. **Typographic contrast.** An expressive serif display paired with a clean,
   quiet sans. Hierarchy through scale and weight, not decoration.
4. **Calm structure, bold moments.** Generous whitespace and rhythm; the drama
   comes from type scale and full-bleed imagery, not busy chrome.
5. **Consistency via tokens.** Every page imports one design system. No more
   per-page divergence.
